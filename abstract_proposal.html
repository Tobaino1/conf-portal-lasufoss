Submission of Abstracts and Full Papers

Deadlines for submission of abstracts and full paper
10th September 2019, for proposal
19th October, 2019 for submission of full papers

The Conference Organising Committee welcomes the submission of paper and panel proposals addressing the theme: Africa in the 21st Century: Development and Challenges, under the following subthemes. This list is not exhaustive; other equally more important interventions on the development question and challenges of Africa will be accepted. Papers are welcome from both theoretical and empirical perspectives from any of the following subthemes and more:

Subthemes

Subtheme A: Economic Development, Corruption, Manpower Development

i. Achieving the knowledge driven economy in Africa: challenges and strategies
ii. International migration and remittance
iii. Strategic manpower challenges
iv. Global Crises and New Challenges of Human Development
v. Poverty and inequality in the 21st century
vi. Governance and corruption security challenges
vii. Food clothing, Shelter and Protection crisis
viii. Strategic manpower challenges



Subtheme B: Geography, Climatic Issues and Transportation

i. Water Shortage and Sanitation Issues in the 21st century
ii. Climate change and variability challenges in the 21st century African development
iii. Transportation and mobility challenges in Africa
iv. Cultural and Ecotourism development
v. Science, ICT Innovation and Africa development
vi. Remote sensing and GIS application challenges in mapping Africa
vii. Sustainable urban and regional planning in Africa



Subtheme C: Politics, Conflicts, Media, Human Security, and Philosophy

i. Manifestations and contestations in Africa-China relations
ii. Amnesty as a conflict Resolution mechanism in Africa
iii. Democracy, democratisation and elections in Africa
iv. Pan-Africanism and decolonisation in contemporary Africa
v. Rethinking Marxism in Africa vi. Conceptual and theoretical issues in Africa development
vii. Philosophy and history of Africa development viii. Religion and African development
ix. The media and the dilemma of image creation in Africa
x. Language as a major barrier in Africa development
xi. Human security and challenges of the 21st century in Africa
xii. Insecurity and leadership challenges in Africa



Subtheme D: Psychology and Mental health

i. Psychopathological problems of the 21st century
ii. Psychological and Environmental behaviour in the 21st century
iii. Psychology of hate speeches
iv. Psychology of national security and national value
v. Challenges of Mental wellbeing



Subtheme E: Sociology, Population and Gender Issues


i. African child mortality
ii. Ageing problems in Africa
iii. Youth involvement in Cybercrimes
iv. Demographic issues in Africa
v. Youth Unemployment and Poverty in Africa
vi. Precarious employment in Africa
vii. Decent work and trade unionism in Africa
viii. Gender issues in workplace
ix. Women and domestic violence
x. Gender and development challenges in Africa


The conference will present distinguished keynote speakers with special panels. Workshops and symposia will also be held for Postgraduate students with other special sessions
